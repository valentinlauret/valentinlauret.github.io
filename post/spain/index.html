Understanding conservation conflicts in Spanish farmland areas

Oct 27, 2017
Avutarda común (Otis tarda) en los campos de Ciurdad Real, Spain

Rodent outbreaks in Northern Spain

During Autumn 2017, I did an internship with Beatriz Arroyo, François Mougeot, and Miguel Delibes-Mateos at IREC (CSIC - UCLM) in Ciudad Real, Spain.

We aimed at using the quali-quantitative social science method named the Q-methodology to describe shared viewpoints about Common vole (Microtus arvalis) outbreak in Northern Spain. During this project, I conducted social interviews with farmers, hunters, conservationists, and members of the local government to idenitfy clusters of shared opinions about this intense conservation conflict.

More on this article in AMBIO journal. To have more details, do not hesitate to contact me directly.
